This draft paper frames \system\ as a staged research program for
evidence-grounded question answering with controlled abstention.
The current implemented contribution is a narrow Stage 1 study that compares an
answer-only baseline with an abstain-aware alternative under a controlled
SQuAD~2.0 setting.

The current Stage 1 evidence is encouraging but mixed in the right way for a
first controlled result.
The abstain-aware model substantially improves unsupported-answer behavior and
decision-aware overall performance, but it still gives up too much answerable
question quality.
That means the project has cleared its first bar: abstention supervision is
useful enough to justify the line of work.
At the same time, the result sharply motivates the next stages, especially
support verification and calibration, because they offer plausible ways to keep
the abstention gain while recovering lost answer quality.
